Please pay full attention to the following and take it rigidly because it defines the fundemental fund flow, lifecycle, and account treatments of the entire fund movement in this applications. 
1- Members transfer money to oner or more external bank account.
2. Regularly, the admin imports bank transactions statement into the the master bank account. System need to be able to identify duplicates of transactions and keep track of imported bank transaction statement. The imported bank statement is a CSV file whose format are defined via a template created and defined and customized in the settings page.
3. Selected transactions (usually, member's contributions/deposits, loan repayments, or loan disbursement) are mirrored to the master cash account.
4. The admin, next posts these transaction to members cash accounts. Posting here means reflecting the funds in the member's cash account as credits without making an actual transfer by the debiting the master cash account (just a mirror of the credit). This is the flow for credits made in the master cash account and they are usually either advances, Contributions, or repayments made by members. Debits (negative amount) in the master cash account are loan disbursements (more on this later).
5. A contribution cycle for a given month starts on a particular day (default the 6th) of the month (configurable via the settings page) and ends the day before the starts of the following cycle (default the 5th).
5. When the admin run the contribution cycle collection (only one contribution or a repayment is allowed per member), the elected fund amount is transfered from the member's cash account to his fund account and mirrored to the master fund account. The transfer to the member's fund account is done by debit/credit transactions.
6. The master fund account reflects the total value of the fund (the sum of all contributions, repayments, minus loan disbursements). The value of the member's fund account is allowed to go negative (indicating that the member has loan to be repaid). Member's may have only one active loan at any given time.
7. The master cash account reflects the totak cash at hand (the sum of all members cash accounts). It increase/decrease when new transactions are imported from the master bank account.
8. When a loan is disbursed to a member, the master fund account is debited for the full disbursement amount. The member's fund account is also debited by the same amount. The disbursed amount is credited to the member's cash account.
9. Member's recive the loan amount by debiting the member's cash account (reflected by also debiting the master cash account). This is matched by a future import of bank statement into the bank account.
